Origin of India Vedic Astrology

Astrology is ‌predictive science and has been in practise since time immemorial in India. It is believed that both Indian astronomy and astrology developed together. The Bhrigu Samhita is the earliest record of Jyotisha (astrology). It was written by Maharishi Bhrigu during the Vedic era. Bhirgu Maharishi is also called the "Father of Vedic Astrology." He is one of the seven venerated Saptarishi or Vedic sages.

Astrology or jyotiṣa is listed as a Vedanga, or branch of the Vedas of the Vedic religion. The only work of this class to have survived is the Vedanga Jyotisha (as much of the text is lost), which explains rules for tracking the motions of the sun and the moon in the context of a five-year intercalation cycle. The date of this important astrological work is uncertain, but the language and the content indicate that it is among the oldest astrological studies in the world.

The Samhita (Compilation) is a text written on pages of tree bark that are said to contain the horoscopes of five million people who have lived in the past or will live in the future.
